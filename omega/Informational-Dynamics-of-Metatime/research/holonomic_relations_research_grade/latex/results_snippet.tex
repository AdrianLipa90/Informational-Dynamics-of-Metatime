\section{Synthetic results}

The synthetic Monte Carlo experiment used $T=2500$ steps and
$N=300$ trajectories per condition.

For the low-distortion condition, the mean final holonomy density was
$8.8408$ with mean $\Theta=0.9400$.
For the high-distortion condition, the mean final holonomy density was
$8.8396$ with mean $\Theta=0.4500$.

These results are consistent with the intended qualitative behavior of the model:
higher distortion leaves a larger residual holonomy defect.
