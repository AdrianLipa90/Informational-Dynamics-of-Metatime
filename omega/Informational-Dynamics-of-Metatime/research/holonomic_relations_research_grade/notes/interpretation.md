# Interpretation

## What the synthetic results mean

The simulation is designed to test a limited methodological claim:

- when coherence dynamics dominates and distortion is low, the model tends to reduce the holonomy defect;
- when distortion is increased, the final holonomy defect tends to remain higher;
- the semantic truth scalar Theta separates the two regimes by construction and should be interpreted as an audited score, not as an oracle.

## What the synthetic results do not mean

They do not demonstrate:
- empirical validity,
- physical truth,
- universality of the formalism,
- uniqueness of the chosen projection or potential.

They only show that the chosen equations generate the intended qualitative behavior.
