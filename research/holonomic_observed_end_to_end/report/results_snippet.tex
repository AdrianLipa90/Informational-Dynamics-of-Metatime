\section{Observed-data end-to-end analysis}

We analysed three observed HaluEval splits under a proxy phase encoding of the Holonomic Relations formalism.
The phase variables were not directly observed; instead, they were constructed from lexical alignment, source-support, and hallucination labels.

For the general split, the mean holonomy defect was 0.219 for non-hallucinated responses and 2.129 for hallucinated responses.
For QA, the corresponding means were 0.254 for truthful answers and 2.219 for hallucinated answers.
For summarization, the means were 0.008 and 2.783, respectively.

Across all three splits, the hallucinated condition exhibited a higher mean holonomy defect than the truthful or non-hallucinated condition under the chosen encoding.
This result supports directional consistency of the observable pipeline, but does not establish uniqueness or empirical necessity of the encoding.
