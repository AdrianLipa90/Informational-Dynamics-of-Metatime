\[
\gamma_i^{\mathrm{eff}} = \phi_i + \gamma_{B,i}.
\]

Here $\phi_i$ is the local sector phase evolved by the relational Lagrangian, while
$\gamma_{B,i}$ is the accumulated Berry contribution generated by motion on the Bloch sphere.

### 2. Pairwise Berry phase and projective distance

\[
\Omega_{ij} = \frac{1}{2}\bigl(1-\cos \bar\theta_{ij}\bigr)\,\Delta\phi_{ij},
\qquad
\bar\theta_{ij}=\frac{\theta_i+\theta_j}{2}.
\]

\[

\]
where $d_{\mathbb D}$ is the Poincare-disk geodesic used by the orbital metric layer.

### 3. Tau-resonant transport kernel
\[
A_{ij}
=
\bigl(w_{ij}\,\mathcal T_{ij}\bigr)
\exp\!\left[i\bigl(\beta\,\Omega_{ij}-\gamma\,d_{ij}\bigr)\right],

\[
\mathcal T_{ij} = \exp\!\left[-\frac12\left(\frac{\log(\tau_i/\tau_j)}{\sigma}\right)^2\right].
\]

This shows explicitly that $A_{ij}$ is not a free coupling: its amplitude is tied to the
compatibility of the sectoral $\tau$-modes and its phase is tied to Berry transport and
hyperbolic separation.

The implemented closure check is
\[
\sum_j A_{ij}\tau_j + A_{i\zeta}\,\tau_{\zeta} \approx e^{i\gamma_i^{\mathrm{eff}}},

where $A_{i\zeta}$ is the zeta-pole contribution and $\tau_{\zeta}$ is the effective zeta
mode.
